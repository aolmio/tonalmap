🟢 Live Syllables (คำเป็น)
Syllables that end with an uninhibited vocal pathway, allowing the sound to naturally sustain.

How to Identify:
  • Ends with a Long Vowel with no final consonant stop (e.g., มา - maa).
  • Ends with a continuous sonorant ringing nasal track: -n (น, ณ, ญ, ร, ล, ฬ), -m (ม), -ng (ง).
  • Ends with semi-vowel vocalic diphthongs: -w (ว), -y (ย).
🔴 Dead Syllables (คำตาย)
Syllables that arrest the air column abruptly, ending with a sharp change in pressure.

How to Identify:
  • Ends with a Short Vowel and lacks any following consonant stop (e.g., จะ - ja).
  • Ends with an unreleased abrupt plosive stop consonant: -k (ก, ข, ค, ฆ), -p (บ, ป, พ, ฟ, ภ), -t (ด, ต, ถ, ท, ธ, ศ, ษ, ส...).
🎵 The ห Leading Rule
When ห (high class initial) directly leads a low class single sonorant consonant like ม, น, ง, ว, ล, ย, ร, the ห acts as a structural class elevator. The combined pair behaves strictly as a single High Class consonant cluster!

This structural mutation explains why the word หมา (dog) resolves directly to a Rising Tone ˇ, rather than a standard flat Mid Tone —.

🪞 The Mid Class Mirror Mid Anchor —
Mid class consonants are incredibly stable anchors. Whatever tone mark icon is drawn over a Mid-class consonant, that exact requested tone sound matches up perfectly (Ek makes Low Tone ˋ, Tho makes Falling ˆ, Tri makes High Tone ˊ, Chattawa makes Rising ˇ).
🏴‍☠️ The Low Class Rebel Shift Frequency Jump
Low class initials act like rebels when modulated by tone mark tags. They shift the actual audio output frequency up by exactly one track setting higher than traditional rules: Mai Ek (่) forces a Falling ˆ tone outcome, and Mai Tho (้) forces a High Tone ˊ outcome.
💥 The Dead Syllable Choke-Spike Pressure Rule
Unmarked Low Class consonants crashing directly into abrupt short vowels (like รัก) don't have safe exit space to fade out. This sudden compression spikes pressure, launching the pitch into the High Tone ˊ zone. Long vowels give it tracking room to climb up and slide back down (Falling ˆ Tone).
🍏 The Baseline Syllable Checklist Foundations
Live Ending (คำเป็น): Finishes natively in smooth long vowels or clear ringing nasal tracks (-m, -n, -ng). Sound waves carry on via a flat baseline Mid Tone — channel unless forced by parameters.
Dead Ending (คำตาย): Chokes off hard into stop consonants (-p, -t, -k) or short vowel constraints. The sound wave hits an abrupt wall causing instant drops to Low Tone ˋ or jumps to High Tone ˊ.
