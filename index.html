📈 Pitch Movement Contour
Consonants are structured into Mid (9), High (11), and Low (24) classes.
■ Mid Class (อักษรกลาง)
■ High Class (อักษรสูง)
■ Low Class (อักษรต่ำ)
Thai vowels are defined by duration length.
■ Short Tracks (สระเสียงสั้น)
■ Long Counterparts (สระเสียงยาว)
Syllables dictate how speech paths naturally dissolve or lock down structural air compression waves.
🟢 Live Syllables (คำเป็น)
Ends with long vowel without stop, or ringing nasals: -n,-m,-ng,-w,-y.
🔴 Dead Syllables (คำตาย)
Ends with short vowel alone, or abrupt plosive stops: -k,-p,-t.
🎵 The ห Leading Rule
When ห directly leads a low class sonorant (ม น ง ว ล ย ร), ห acts as a class elevator — the pair becomes a single High Class cluster. E.g. หมา → Rising Tone ˇ, not Mid.
🔵 The อ Leading Rule (อ นำ)
When อ (mid class) leads a low class consonant like ย — e.g. in the word อยู่ (yuu = "to stay") — the silent อ elevates the low class ย to behave as Mid Class. This is why อยู่ with Mai Ek produces Low Tone (not Falling), matching mid class + Mai Ek rules.

Key อ Leading Pairs: อย (ya), อว (wa). Watch for silent อ at syllable onset.

🪞 The Mid Class Mirror Anchor —
Mid class consonants are stable anchors. Whatever tone mark appears over a Mid-class consonant, that exact tone sounds perfectly (Ek→Low, Tho→Falling, Tri→High, Chattawa→Rising).
🏴‍☠️ The Low Class Rebel Shift Frequency Jump
Low class initials with tone marks shift one track higher: Mai Ek (่) → Falling, Mai Tho (้) → High.
💥 Dead Syllable Choke-Spike Pressure Rule
Unmarked Low Class + short vowel dead ending → High Tone ˊ (pressure spike). Long vowel dead → Falling ˆ.
🍏 Baseline Syllable Checklist Foundations
Live (คำเป็น): Long vowel or nasal ending → Mid Tone baseline.
Dead (คำตาย): Stop consonant or short vowel → Low or High depending on class.
🎵 ห Leading Elevator
ห + low class sonorant (ม น ง ว ล ย ร) → acts as High Class. หมา = rising ˇ, หนาว = rising ˇ, หวาน = rising ˇ.
🔵 อ Leading Silencer (อ นำ)
Silent อ (mid class) before ย or ว elevates them to Mid Class behavior. อยู่ (yuu) with Mai Ek → Low Tone. อย่าง (yaang) → Low Tone. Remember: the อ is silent but controls the class!
🕳️ The Invisible Vowel Rule
Thai frequently omits the short inherent vowel /o/ or /a/ between consonants. คน = kh+o+n (the 'o' is invisible). กระ = g+r+a (the 'a' is implicit). When no vowel marker appears between two consonants, assume a short inherent vowel is hiding there.
📈 Pitch Contour Memory Aid
Mid —: Flat line, no movement.
Low ˋ: Slightly below mid, gently descends.
Falling ˆ: Starts high, curves sharply down.
High ˊ: Starts mid, rises high, holds.
Rising ˇ: Dips low first, then climbs up.
