🟢 Live Syllables (คำเป็น)
Syllables that end with an uninhibited vocal pathway, allowing the sound to naturally sustain.

How to Identify:
  • Ends with a Long Vowel with no final consonant stop (e.g., มา - maa).
  • Ends with a continuous sonorant ringing nasal track: -n (น, ณ, ญ, ร, ล, ฬ), -m (ม), -ng (ง).
  • Ends with semi-vowel vocalic diphthongs: -w (ว), -y (ย).
🔴 Dead Syllables (คำตาย)
Syllables that arrest the air column abruptly, ending with a sharp change in pressure waves.

How to Identify:
  • Ends with a Short Vowel and lacks any following consonant stop (e.g., จะ - ja).
  • Ends with an unreleased abrupt plosive stop consonant: -k (ก, ข, ค, ฆ), -p (บ, ป, พ, ฟ, ภ), -t (ด, ต, ถ, ท, ธ, ศ, ษ, ส...).
🎵 The Leading Consonants Modifier Rules (ห Leading & อ Leading)
Thai utilizes leading modifiers to shift baseline classes of low sonorant track elements:

• ห Leading (ห นำ): When ห (High Class) leads low class single sonorants (ม, น, ง, ว, ล, ย, ร, ญ), the cluster inherits High Class execution paths completely (e.g., หมา transforms Mid Tone baseline to Rising Tone).

• อ Leading (อ นำ): When อ (Mid Class) leads the sonorant character ย, it restricts operations entirely to Mid Class parsing parameters. This exists exclusively in 4 specialized core vocabulary elements: อย่า (Do not), อยู่ (To reside), อย่าง (Type/Like), อยาก (To desire).

🪞 The Mid Class Mirror Mid Anchor —
Mid class consonants are stable anchors. Whatever tone mark icon is drawn over a Mid-class consonant, that exact requested tone sound matches up perfectly.
🏴‍☠️ The Low Class Rebel Shift Frequency Jump
Low class initials act like rebels when modulated by tone mark tags. They shift the audio output frequency up by exactly one track setting higher than traditional rules: Mai Ek (่) forces a Falling ˆ tone outcome, and Mai Tho (้) forces a High Tone ˊ outcome.
💥 The Dead Syllable Choke-Spike Pressure Rule
Unmarked Low Class consonants crashing directly into abrupt short vowels (like รัก) don't have safe exit space to fade out. This sudden compression spikes pressure, launching the pitch into the High Tone ˊ zone.
